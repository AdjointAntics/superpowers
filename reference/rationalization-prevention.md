# Rationalization Prevention

Reference for recognizing and countering the rationalizations that cause skill/process skipping.

## The Pattern

"I'm about to skip a required step because [rationalization]."

Every rationalization below feels reasonable in the moment. That is what makes them dangerous.

## The Five Rationalizations

**1. "This is too simple to need the skill."**
Simple tasks are where unexamined assumptions waste the most work. Complexity is obvious and forces caution. Simplicity breeds overconfidence. Check the skill.

**2. "I'll just do this one thing first."**
The skill tells you HOW to do things -- including what to do first. If you're about to act without checking, you've already decided the skill doesn't apply. Check before doing.

**3. "I already know how to do this."**
Skills evolve. Your memory may be stale. The current version may contain steps, constraints, or context you don't remember. Read the current version, not your memory of it.

**4. "This feels productive."**
Feeling productive and being productive are different things. Undisciplined action generates rework. Process prevents wasted motion. If it feels urgent to skip the process, that's the strongest signal to follow it.

**5. "The process is overkill for this."**
If the skill exists and applies to what you're doing, use it. The skill was written by someone who already considered whether it was overkill. That's the 1% rule: spend 1% of your time checking the skill to avoid wasting 99% on preventable mistakes.

## The Counter

When you catch yourself rationalizing: stop, read the skill, then proceed. Every time.
